In the spring of 1922, on a small farmstead known as **Hinterkaifeck**, roughly
70 kilometers north of Munich, six people were killed in a single night. The
victims were a farming family and their maid. Nearly a hundred years of
investigation, exhumation, and renewed forensic interest have never produced a
conviction.

## The days before

In the week leading up to the killings, the family reported strange events.
Footprints were found in the snow leading from the edge of the forest to the
house — but none leading back. The patriarch told neighbors he had heard
footsteps in the attic and found a newspaper in the house that no one had
brought home. A set of house keys went missing. The family's previous maid had
reportedly left, telling others she believed the house was haunted.

The new maid arrived to begin work on the very day the family is believed to
have been killed.

## What was found

When concerned neighbors finally entered the property days later, they
discovered the bodies. Four of the victims had been led, one by one, into the
barn and killed; two more were found inside the house. The murder weapon was
believed to be a **mattock**, a pickaxe-like farming tool.

The most chilling detail emerged afterward: evidence suggested the killer or
killers had **remained at the farm for several days** after the murders.
Neighbors had seen smoke rising from the chimney. The livestock had been fed.
Someone had eaten in the kitchen.

## A century of theories

Investigators questioned dozens of people over the decades. Theories have ranged
from robbery to family disputes to a stranger who had been living, undetected,
on the property. The farmhouse was demolished in 1923. In the 2000s, students at
a Bavarian police academy re-examined the case using modern methods and reached
a conclusion about a likely suspect — but, out of respect for living relatives,
declined to release the name publicly, and no charges were ever possible.

The Hinterkaifeck murders endure precisely because they resist resolution: a
crime witnessed by no one, committed by someone who felt comfortable enough to
stay.
